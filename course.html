What is Applied Mathematics?

According to Northwestern University, applied mathematics refers to the use of mathematics to solve issues that come up in a variety of fields, such as science, engineering, or other broad fields, as well as the creation of novel or enhanced solutions to problems that present new difficulties.


We define applied mathematics as the use of mathematics to solve practical issues in order to both explain and predict previously unseen events. Thus, the focus is on the actual world as well as mathematics, such as the creation of innovative techniques to tackle novel issues.


The applications in the physical and biological sciences, engineering, and social sciences are where the problems originate. In order to solve them, one must be knowledgeable in a number of mathematical fields, including analysis, differential equations, and stochastics, using both analytical and numerical techniques. In order to witness the outcomes of their study come to life, our academic members and students frequently work directly with experimentalists.


Furthermore, applied mathematics involves the application of mathematics to real-world problems which arise in various areas. BS Applied Mathematics is a program offered by the College of Arts and Sciences through the Department of Physical Sciences. It has balance between a holistic general education program and a substantial mathematics curriculum. Nonetheless, this program provides a strong foundation in different mathematical techniques and their application in various fields. The program aims to train students and develop them to become competent and well-equipped instructors to teach mathematics in college and also to prepare them for mathematics oriented career in industry, business and public administration.
